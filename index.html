China,the new No.1 tourism market

Source:The World Bank

In 2005 China ranked seventh in international tourism expenditure and from then on, Chinese outbound tourism boomed rapidly. It successfully overtook Italy, Japan, France and the United Kingdom.Year 2013 witnessed China leap to the first place, surpassing both top spender United States and second largest spender Germany.

According to China Tourism Research Institute, China had 120 million outbound visitors in 2015 and they spent 104.5 billion US doallars, which has already far surpassed some countries' household consumption expenditure, for example, Indonesia and Turkey.However, China's per capital travel expenditure ranks far behind other countries.

Chinese travelers' favorite destinations

From the perspective of geographical convenience, countries like Japan, Korea and other southeast countries in Asia are always the most popular places for Chinese outbound travelers. More airlines connect between China and these countries, less time to apply visa to these countries, cheap but high-quality products in these countries are parts of the reason behind this phenomenon.

Moreover, due to convenience of transportation, similar culture and special tourism policy, Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan, are the top cities that Chinese outbound travelers prefer to go shopping. Since Authorized organizations from the Chinese mainland and Taiwan exchanged written documents in 2011 to confirm the schedule of a pilot travel program that will allow mainlanders to visit Taiwan as individual tourists, more and more young Chinese people travel to Taiwan.

Source:China National Tourism Administration

Demographics of Chinese travelers

In China, the Millennial Generation takes the stage now. Compared to their parents, they have more passion to travel abroad to broaden their view. Since they grew up in a comparatively affluent period, they are more better- educated, open-minded, technology sophisticated, and media savvy. In the meantime, they like shopping and don’t think much about input and output.

Over half of them are post-80s
China's post-80s, also called Millennial Generation, are the first generation since China’s one-child policy was born. They witnessed China’s opening up and enjoy the benefits of Chinese economic reform. They were labeled as “rebellious”, “spoiled” and “self-centered”. Now, most of them are fighting for their career and feeding their own family. They are taking the stage in China.
Family tours are quite popular currently
In post-80s family and post-90s family, parents focus more on their children. Thus family tours with minor children have featured in Chinese outbound tourism as a way to broaden their horizons.
They are still in the higher income groups
Tourists with a higher monthly income take over 6 days to travel.The individual monthly income of tourists traveling to Europe and US is higher than that of those visiting Japan, South Korea or Southeast Asia.Most group tourists earn less than 1235 dollars per month and the monthly income of most independent tourists is over 1544 dollars.
They are quite generous in consumption
The more tourists earn, the more they spend while traveling abroad. Per capita spending of tourists in cities in Europe and US is the highest, followed by cities in Japan and South Korea. The 3rd ranking goes to cities in Southeast Asia.Overseas spending of independent tourists ranks the first, followed by semi-independent and group tourists.
Shopping is the item they spend the most
Over half of the tourists (57.76%) mentioned that their travel budgets mainly focused on shopping.Chinese tourists in Japan and South Korea spend most on shopping while those in Southeast Asia spend it on travel and entertainment. Those in Europe and US spend on lodging, tickets, luxury goods and souvenirs.

Find their traveling story

During 2013 and 2014, Chinese outbound tourists were mainly from South China, North China and East China; Shanghai, Guangdong and Beijing have the most overseas tourists, followed by coastal provinces such as Jiangsu, Zhejiang and Shandong. Liaoning in Northeast China, Hubei in Central China and Sichuan in Southwest China are the provinces with a large tourist flow to other countries.Influenced by economic development, the regional distribution of Chinese outbound tourists reveals huge difference between eastern cities and western cities, between inland cities and costal cities. As a result, the travelers from different area of China, have different outbound travel preference, travel frequency and travel stories.
